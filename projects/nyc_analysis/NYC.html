Project Background

Why this report?
Motor vehicle collisions are a leading cause of injury and death in New York City. This analysis leverages NYPD open data to reveal patterns in accident timing, locations, and root causes, with the goal of informing public safety and urban planning efforts.

Executive Summary

  • 230,000+ collisions analyzed (Jan 2021 – Apr 2023)
  • 116,227 injuries and 635 deaths
  • Brooklyn is the most dangerous borough (29% of casualties)
  • Friday is the riskiest day (18% of deaths)
  • July has the highest fatality rate (summer spike)

Contributing Factors: What Causes the Most Fatalities?

The chart below shows the top contributing factors to fatal collisions. Unsafe speed and driver inattention are the leading causes, with alcohol involvement and failure to yield also significant.

Actionable insight: Targeted enforcement and education on speeding, distraction, and impaired driving could save lives.

Key Insights

  • Friday has the highest percentage of deaths (18%) and injuries (15%) among all days.
  • Afternoon rush hour (4–6 PM) sees the most injuries, while late night/early morning (12–5 AM) is deadliest.
  • Unsafe speed is the leading cause of fatal crashes, followed by driver inattention and unknown factors.
  • Alcohol involvement and failure to yield are also significant contributors.

Recommendations

  • Increase enforcement and awareness on Fridays and during late-night hours.
  • Target speed enforcement and distracted driving campaigns in high-risk areas.
  • Install more traffic calming measures and improve pedestrian safety infrastructure.
  • Promote public education on the dangers of speeding and impaired driving.
